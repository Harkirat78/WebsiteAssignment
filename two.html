Meatball Sub

A delicious sandwich made with homemade meatballs and marinara sauce, topped with melted mozzarella cheese and served on a toasted roll.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb ground beef
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped parsley
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper
  • 2 cups marinara sauce
  • 4 hoagie rolls
  • 8 oz mozzarella cheese, shredded

Directions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375Â°F. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, combine ground beef, breadcrumbs, milk, Parmesan cheese, parsley, egg, salt, and black pepper. Mix until well combined.
  3. Roll the mixture into 1 1/2-inch meatballs and place them on the prepared baking sheet. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until cooked through.
  4. While the meatballs are baking, heat the marinara sauce in a large saucepan over medium heat.
  5. When the meatballs are done, add them to the sauce and stir to coat.
  6. Split the hoagie rolls and place them on a baking sheet. Top each roll with the meatballs and sauce, then sprinkle with shredded mozzarella cheese.
  7. Bake for 5-7 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.

Plant-Based Meat Curry and Spinach

This flavorful curry is made with a meat substitute and spinach in a spiced tomato sauce. It's perfect served over rice or with naan bread.

Ingredients:

  • 1 tbsp oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tbsp grated ginger
  • 1 tbsp curry powder
  • 1/2 tsp ground cumin
  • 1/2 tsp ground coriander
  • 1/4 tsp cayenne pepper
  • 1 package (12 oz) meat substitute, such as Beyond Meat or Tofurky, crumbled
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes, undrained
  • 2 cups fresh spinach
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper

Directions:

  1. In a large skillet, heat oil over medium heat. Add onion and cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add garlic and ginger and cook for 1-2 minutes, until fragrant.
  3. Add curry powder, cumin, coriander, and cayenne pepper. Stir to coat the onions and cook for 1 minute.
  4. Add the crumbled meat substitute and diced tomatoes with their juices. Bring to a simmer and let cook for 10-15 minutes, until the sauce has thickened slightly.
  5. Stir in the spinach and let cook for 1-2 minutes, until wilted.
  6. Season with salt and pepper, to taste.
  7. Serve over rice or with naan bread.
